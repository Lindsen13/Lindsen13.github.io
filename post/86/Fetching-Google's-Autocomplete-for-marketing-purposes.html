Fetching Google's Autocomplete for marketing purposes

2020-07-27 • By Ivo

Automate keyword generation and SEO tools: Google's Autocomplete in your terminal!

I often come across self-proclaimed dropshipping-kings on Facebook and other social media. They try to convince their followers how to make thousands of dollars without doing anything. Yeah right.

I will show you a little trick that might be able to help you with your marketing quest. Namely: A simple function that retrieves google's autocomplete list. Let's say you want to find out what people are looking for on google for your marketing campaign, the following might help you.

import requests

def search_results(search_query):
    url = f'http://suggestqueries.google.com/complete/search?client=safari&q={search_query}'
    output = requests.get(url)
    if output.status_code == 200:
        for element in output.json()[1]:
            print(element[0])

search_results('python automation')

Curious how you're able to leverage this information. Perhaps we can stretch it even more by calling the search_results() function for each result in the first function!

def search_results_deeper(search_query):
    url = f'http://suggestqueries.google.com/complete/search?client=safari&q={search_query}'
    output = requests.get(url)
    if output.status_code == 200:
        for element in output.json()[1]:
            search_results(element[0])
